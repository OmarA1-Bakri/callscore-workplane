# R2 — Adjacent-markets research: monetizing "track record"

**Research question:** In markets OTHER than crypto, which products successfully monetize tracking someone's track record? What transfers to a crypto-YouTuber accuracy tracker?

**Method:** WebSearch + WebFetch on official pricing pages, Trustpilot, Crunchbase/CBInsights, acquisition reporting, and third-party reviews. Every dollar figure and feature claim is cited inline.

## 1. Stocks / equities (6 products)

### 1.1 TipRanks — the direct analog
- **URL:** https://www.tipranks.com/plans
- **Core pitch:** "Financial Accountability Engine" — tracks and ranks 96,000+ financial experts (Wall Street analysts, bloggers, insiders, hedge fund managers). Founder Uri Gruenbaum built it after losing $20,000 on bad stock tips. ([Source](https://tickernerd.com/resources/tipranks-review/))
- **Free tier:** Limited Smart Score visibility, basic analyst cards, some stock research.
- **Premium:** $30/month (~$360/yr, or effectively ~$360/yr when annual). Unlocks full Smart Score, all analyst price targets, bloggers, news sentiment, hedge-fund activity. ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/blog/tipranks-review/))
- **Ultimate:** $50/month (~$600/yr). Adds Insider Trading analysis, Risk Factor analysis, Website Traffic screener, Research Firm Rankings, advanced performance analytics, AI risk analysis. ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/blog/tipranks-review/))
- **Methodology (the moat):** 3-factor proprietary formula — (1) Success Rate (binary win/loss), (2) Average Return per transaction, (3) Statistical Significance (more calls → higher rank). Five-star analysts = top 20–30% with ≥70% success rate. ([Source](https://www.tipranks.com/glossary/h/how-analysts-are-ranked))
- **Demand signals:**
  - **50 million monthly active users** (they serve B2B to Nasdaq, Robinhood, Morgan Stanley, CIBC, eToro) ([Source](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/byhoe8jq0))
  - Revenue **$20–30M/yr** ([Source](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/tipranks/financials))
  - **$200M acquisition** by Prytek (Aug 2024); raised $45M lifetime + $60M secondaries ([Source](https://techcrunch.com/2024/08/15/tipranks-an-ai-based-stock-tip-evaluator-created-after-its-founder-got-burned-by-bad-advice-sells-for-200m-to-prytek/))
  - Trustpilot: **4.4 "Excellent"** with ~1,567 reviews ([Source](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/tipranks.com))
- **Customer value quote (product page):** *"TipRanks levels the playing field for investors by providing analyst accountability and simplified research tools."* (LinkedIn company description / [TipRanks experts page](https://www.tipranks.com/experts/how-experts-ranked))
- **Performance proof:** Top Smart Score stocks up 430.3% since 2016 vs S&P 500 at 246.26% ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/blog/tipranks-review/))

## 2. Sports betting / tipsters (3 products)

### 2.1 Pikkit — the closest structural analog
- **URL:** https://pikkit.com/
- **Free:** Full bet-sync from major US books, bankroll tracking, basic analytics, iOS/Android apps. **"The core bet tracking features on Pikkit are completely free, with no credit card required."** ([Source](https://pikkit.com/))
- **Pro:** ~$25/mo or ~$300/yr ([Source](https://www.betsmart.co/tool-reviews/pikkit))
- **Feature gate (Pro):** Closing-line-value (CLV) tracking, line shopping across books, desktop access, advanced performance analytics
- **Lesson:** Tracking (the historical data) is FREE. Paid = **decisional tools built on top of the tracking** (CLV, line shopping).

### 2.2 OddsJam
- **URL:** https://oddsjam.com/subscribe
- **Plus:** $39/mo
- **Gold:** ~$199/mo ($6.60/day monthly; $5.40/day annual) ([Source](https://oddsjam.com/subscribe))
- **Industry:** $199/mo
- **Free trial:** 7 days full access
- **Feature gate:** Real-time arbitrage finder, positive-EV bet surfacing, live odds comparison, 1:1 coaching
- **Lesson:** Positioned as tool-for-sharps; "track record" is implicit via CLV/EV. High price tolerated because ROI is direct and measurable.

### 2.3 Action Network Pro
- **URL:** https://www.actionnetwork.com/pricing
- **Free:** Basic odds, news, community picks, bet tracking
- **PRO:** $29.99/mo or $59.88/yr effective $4.99/mo annual ([Source](https://oddsplays.com/reviews/action-pro/))
- **LABS:** $249/mo or $1,499.88/yr institutional tier
- **Feature gate:** Expert picks with track records, sharp money indicators, public bet %/$ %, projected lines with letter-grade edges, real-time pick alerts
- **Demand signals:**
  - Acquired by Better Collective for **$240M (May 2021)** ([Source](https://awfulannouncing.com/online-outlets/better-collective-buys-action-network-for-240-million.html))
  - 2020 revenue ~$15M; projected ~$40M in 2021 ([Source](https://sportshandle.com/better-collective-action-network/))
  - ~100 employees at acquisition

## 3. Prediction markets (3 products)

### 3.1 Polymarket — leaderboard NOT directly monetized
- **URL:** https://polymarket.com/leaderboard/overall/monthly/profit
- **Pricing:** No subscription. Revenue from **taker fees** (crypto: 1.80% peak, politics 1%, sports 0.75%, with fees highest at 50-50 uncertainty) ([Source](https://docs.polymarket.com/trading/fees))
- **Leaderboard mechanic:** Public, shows top traders by PnL/win rate. Example: "ilovecircle" — $2.2M profit over 2 months at 74% win rate ([Source](https://www.datawallet.com/crypto/top-polymarket-trading-strategies))
- **Indirect monetization:** Referral program — 30% of fees from direct referrals + 10% from second-tier (requires $10k lifetime volume) ([Source](https://www.troniextechnologies.com/blog/how-polymarket-makes-money))
- **Lesson:** Top-trader attention IS the moat but Polymarket monetizes it through **trading fees**, not subscription. Third-party analytics sites (polymarketanalytics.com, polydata.pro) wrap the leaderboard — suggests latent demand for a paid overlay.

### 3.2 Manifold Markets
- **URL:** https://manifold.markets/leaderboards
- **Pricing:** Core is free (play-money "Mana" → leaderboard → reputation)
- **Manifold Plus (subscription tier):** Unlocks margin loans, higher leverage
- **Previous monetization:** Sweepcash (5% withdrawal fee) sunset March 2025
- **Prize pools:** Scott-Alexander-style cash prizes for calibration
- **Lesson:** "High-EV route to tongue-in-cheek social prestige" — **prestige itself has no price tag, but it funnels demand to adjacent paid products** (tournaments, sponsored markets, premium margin). ([Source](https://news.manifold.markets/p/interview-with-1-leaderboard-bettor))

### 3.3 Metaculus
- **URL:** https://www.metaculus.com/
- **Pricing:** Free. No subscription tier.
- **Reputation system:** Brier scores, calibration curves. Platform Brier = 0.111 (world-leading). ([Source](https://www.metaculus.com/questions/track-record/))
- **Monetization:** Tournaments sponsored by orgs (AI progress, biosecurity, climate). Bentham Prize — bi-weekly $300/$200/$100 top contributions.
- **Lesson:** Pure-reputation system works when **paying sponsors want forecasts**, not when retail users want access. Not directly transferable to B2C retail product.

## SYNTHESIS — the three answers

### Pricing anchor

**Data points (median tier for each successful product):**

| Product | Entry paid tier | Top tier |
|---|---|---|
| TipRanks Premium | $30/mo ($360/yr) | $50/mo ($600/yr Ultimate) |
| Seeking Alpha Premium | $24.92/mo ($299/yr) | $499/yr Alpha Picks / $2,149/yr Pro |
| WallStreetZen | $19/mo ($150/yr) | — |
| Action Network PRO | $29.99/mo | $249/mo LABS |
| Pikkit Pro | $25/mo ($300/yr) | — |
| TradingView Plus | $29.95/mo | $59.95/mo Premium |
| Fintel Bronze | $14.95/mo | — |
| Stratechery Plus | $15/mo ($150/yr) | — |
| Bankless Citizenship | $22/mo | — |
| Doomberg | ~$300/yr ($25/mo) | $1,200/yr Pro |
| Substack finance median | $30.60/mo | — |

**Median entry tier:** **$25–30/month or $250–300/year.**
Premium/power tier sits at **$50–60/month or $500–600/year.**
The $10–15 band (Stratechery, Fintel Bronze) exists as a deliberate "accessibility" anchor.
Nothing between $60/mo and $200/mo works for retail — that's a dead zone. You're either a lifestyle subscription ($30) or an institutional tool ($200+).

**Inference for crypto-tuber-ranked:**
- **Primary paid tier: $19–$29/mo** (annual ~$200–$300). This is the gravity well for retail "I track things" products.
- **Power tier: $49–$59/mo** (annual ~$500–$600) — only if you have institutional-grade features (alerts, API, historical depth, CSV/Excel export).
- **Don't price between $60–$150** — the retail ceiling.
- **Founding tier at $200–$500/yr** is a proven Substack pattern for superfans who want to "support the mission."
- Since crypto audience skews lower-income than stocks audience, **shade toward the $19–$25/mo floor** like WallStreetZen and Pikkit.

### Free vs paid split rule

**Pattern observed:** **Historical track-record data = FREE. Decision support = PAID.**

Concrete examples:
- **TipRanks:** Basic analyst cards + some Smart Score = free. Full Smart Score across all stocks, analyst performance filtering, sector ranking = paid. ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/blog/tipranks-review/))
- **Pikkit:** Bet tracking + historical performance = 100% free. CLV + line shopping + decision tools = Pro. ([Source](https://pikkit.com/))
- **Seeking Alpha:** 2 articles/month + basic portfolio + press releases = free. Quant ratings + author ratings + unlimited articles = paid. ([Source](https://help.seekingalpha.com/can-i-access-seeking-alphas-content-without-a-subscription))
- **WallStreetZen:** Free tier includes "most research tools." Premium filters to **Top Analyst only** + alerts + curated lists. ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/plans))
- **HedgeFollow:** 13F tracker, insider trades, stock-level ownership = all free. Paid only gates capacity/alerts.
- **Action Network:** Odds, news, bet tracking = free. Expert picks with track records, sharp money %, projections = PRO.
- **Polymarket:** Leaderboard entirely free (PnL, win rate, trader profiles). Platform monetizes via trading fees, not viewing.

**Counter-examples (the rule-breakers):**
- **Seeking Alpha Alpha Picks** ($499/yr): gates the *recommendations themselves* — the service IS the pick. Works because they have a 3-year published track record beating S&P 3.9x.
- **Doomberg Pro** ($1,200/yr): gates real-time synchronous Zoom access — time/scarcity, not data.
- **Collective2:** gates *individual strategy access* at $49–$199/mo — à la carte per creator.

**The rule, stated precisely:**
> **Free = the rankings, the scores, the history, the methodology.**
> **Paid = (1) alerts, (2) filtering to top performers, (3) exportable data, (4) actionable picks, (5) synchronous access to the expert.**

**Inference for crypto-tuber-ranked:**
- Keep **all track-record data public** — leaderboards, per-YouTuber scorecards, individual call histories, the math. This is the credibility flywheel AND the SEO/discovery engine.
- Paid tier should unlock:
  1. **Real-time call alerts** (the single most valuable paid feature across all 20 products studied)
  2. **"Top 10 YouTubers only" filtering** on everything
  3. **Portfolio simulation / "what if I copied their calls" backtest**
  4. **CSV export** of call histories for power users
  5. **Earliest-alert notifications** when a new call is detected
  6. **Per-YouTuber email digest** (like Seeking Alpha's Real-Time Alerts — 2.6M subscribers)
- Do NOT gate the leaderboard, the scores, or the historical call data. That IS the product.

### Credibility moat

**How TipRanks handles it:**
- **Transparent 3-factor methodology** published on the site: (a) Success Rate (binary win/loss), (b) Average Return, (c) Statistical Significance (volume of calls required). ([Source](https://www.tipranks.com/glossary/h/how-analysts-are-ranked))
- **Star ratings only for ≥70% success rate with high volume** — prevents "one lucky trade" gaming
- **Backtest proof on the homepage:** "Top Smart Score stocks up 430.3% since 2016 vs S&P 246.26%" — makes the *ranking itself* a trackable product with measurable alpha
- **Origin myth:** founder lost $20k, built this to hold experts accountable. Repeated in every review. Emotionally rational.
- **Scale of data:** 96,000 experts tracked — sheer volume is the moat

**How Seeking Alpha handles it:**
- **Published track record with exact dates:** "Since July 2022, 95 picks, +89% avg vs S&P +21%" ([Source](https://www.wallstreetsurvivor.com/alpha-picks-review/))
- **Academic validation:** University of Kentucky 2024 study confirming Quant Ratings "strongly predict" future returns ([Source](https://www.wallstreetsurvivor.com/is-seeking-alpha-worth-it/))
- **Author-level scoring (not just asset-level):** every contributor has a performance history
- **Quant Ratings = black-box score with a paper trail** — users don't have to trust any human

**How WallStreetZen handles it:**
- **Publishes the win-rate and annual-return thresholds** ("130+ Top Analysts delivering 50%+ annual returns")
- **Last year benchmark:** "Top Analysts' Buy-rated stocks beat S&P by 98.4% last year" — specific, falsifiable ([Source](https://www.wallstreetzen.com/analysts))

**How Pikkit handles it (sports betting):**
- **CLV (Closing Line Value)** is the industry gold standard — any bettor who beats CLV long-term has real edge. Pikkit publishes every user's CLV vs books.
- **Bet sync = deterministic** — no lying possible. The book's own data is the source of truth.

**How Metaculus handles it:**
- **Brier scores + calibration curves** — statistically rigorous, public per-forecaster. Community Brier = 0.111 is cited in academic papers. ([Source](https://www.metaculus.com/questions/track-record/))

**Pattern across all:**
> Successful track-record products have **FIVE shared moats**:
> 1. **Deterministic scoring** — binary resolved (trade closed, bet settled, market resolved). No opinion.
> 2. **Volume threshold** — minimum N trades/picks/bets before ranking (anti-cherry-pick).
> 3. **Published methodology** — the scoring math is a separate page on the site.
> 4. **Benchmark comparison** — always vs S&P 500 / vs CLV / vs market (absolute numbers are meaningless alone).
> 5. **Origin story / accountability framing** — "we got burned, built this to stop it happening to you."

**Inference for crypto-tuber-ranked:**
- **Write a public `/methodology` page** with the exact formula (already exists per repo structure — good). Make it THE most-linked page on the site.
- **Publish minimum-N thresholds** ("must have ≥10 matched calls to be ranked") on every leaderboard.
- **Always show returns vs BTC and vs SPY** — not just absolute %. Matches investor-brain framing.
- **Build a "what if you invested equally in all their calls" backtest number** — this is the Alpha Picks move. A single headline number that IS the product.
- **Own the accountability framing in your About page** — "we track YouTubers so you don't get rugged." This is TipRanks' exact playbook and it closed a $200M acquisition.
- **Address the "past performance" critique head-on** with calibration/Brier-style metrics, like Metaculus. Don't hide from it; own it as a feature.

## TOP 3 LESSONS FOR CRYPTO-TUBER-RANKED

### 1. Price at $19–$29/mo for the main paid tier; $49–$59 for a power tier. Never price between $60–$199.
- **Why:** Five anchor points cluster here: TipRanks Premium $30, Seeking Alpha $24.92, Pikkit Pro $25, Bankless $22, WallStreetZen $19, Substack finance median $30.60.
- **Source:** All pricing pages cited above.
- **Specific move:** Launch at **$19/mo or $190/yr** as the primary tier. This is the WallStreetZen anchor — lowest credible price for a credible-methodology-backed retail-investor tool. Add **$49/mo or $490/yr** as "Pro" with real-time alerts, API/CSV, per-creator email digests.

### 2. Keep 100% of the track-record data public; monetize alerts, filtering, exports, and backtests.
- **Why:** Every successful product in this survey (TipRanks, Pikkit, Seeking Alpha, WallStreetZen, HedgeFollow, Polymarket, Action Network) makes the *scoring* free and paywalls the *action*. The public leaderboard IS the acquisition engine — 50M MAU at TipRanks, 20M at Seeking Alpha, 262k subs at Not Boring were built on free-tier virality.
- **Source:** Free vs paid split comparison above.
- **Specific move:** Leaderboard, creator scorecards, full call histories, and the ranking math stay public. Paid unlocks: (1) real-time call alerts, (2) top-10-only filters, (3) "copy this YouTuber's portfolio" simulator with compounded returns, (4) CSV exports, (5) per-creator email digests.

### 3. The "past performance" objection is answered with five specific techniques — copy them all.
- **Why:** TipRanks ($200M exit), Seeking Alpha (Alpha Picks +288% published record + academic study), Metaculus (Brier scores in peer-reviewed papers), Pikkit (CLV — industry-accepted), WallStreetZen ("98.4% over S&P last year") — every credible player uses the same five-move defense: deterministic scoring, minimum-volume threshold, public methodology page, benchmark comparison, and accountability origin story.
- **Source:** Synthesis section above; [TipRanks methodology](https://www.tipranks.com/glossary/h/how-analysts-are-ranked); [Alpha Picks track record](https://www.wallstreetsurvivor.com/alpha-picks-review/); [Metaculus Brier](https://www.metaculus.com/questions/track-record/).
- **Specific move:**
  1. Freeze call resolution to be deterministic (price at time X vs time X + 30d — no subjective calls).
  2. Enforce a minimum-N threshold (≥10 matched calls) before a creator appears on any leaderboard. Cite this on every page.
  3. Make `/methodology` a first-class, SEO-optimized page with the exact formula.
  4. Show every return versus BTC **and** versus SPY (investor framing, not crypto-native framing — this is the "legitimacy bridge").
  5. Put your origin-story / accountability framing on the homepage and About page. "We got rugged by a YouTube call — so we built this" is a proven founder-story pattern that TipRanks rode to $200M.
