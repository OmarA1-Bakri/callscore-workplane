I lost $11,400
the month I started
trusting YouTube.

↓ keep reading

Here is what happened to me.

In October 2024 a creator I had been watching for months called a coin he said was “the asymmetric bet of the cycle.” I did what the comments section was doing. I bought at $0.084, averaged up at $0.11, and told myself I was being disciplined by setting a mental stop.

Four days later the chart did the thing charts do when someone paid for a pump: straight up, flat, then a single red candle that ate the whole move. I sold at $0.019. By the time I closed the position I was down $11,400 — most of what I had put aside that year to pay estimated taxes.

The creator never mentioned the call again. The video stayed up. The comments praising his “conviction” stayed up. I went looking for a site that tracked this sort of thing and realized no such site existed in a form I could trust. So I started building one, mostly out of spite.

Here is what I will not do.

  • I will not charge creators.
  • I will not take sponsorships from tracked creators.
  • I will not hide methodology.
  • I will not hide my mistakes.

If I ever stop updating this, assume I sold out.
