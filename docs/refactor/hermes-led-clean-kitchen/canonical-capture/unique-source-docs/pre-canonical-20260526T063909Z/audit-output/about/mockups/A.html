The crypto internet forgets on purpose. We do not.

Every bull run produces the same casualty list: a retail trader, a screenshot of a call that aged badly, and an influencer who has already deleted the tweet and moved on to the next narrative. The Ledger exists because that cycle depends on one thing — your forgetting. We built a machine that does not.

Since launch we have ingested every public call made by twenty of the most followed crypto YouTubers, matched each one to a ticker and a timestamp, and priced it against eighteen‑point‑seven million minute‑level candles sourced directly from exchanges. Every call is scored. Every score is dated. Nothing is retroactively edited.

“Accountability, it turns out, is just arithmetic nobody wanted to do.”

The methodology is deliberately unglamorous. We do not grade on vibes or engagement. A call is either in a matched window, or it is not. Its return over the published horizon is either positive against the market, or it is not. We publish the answer either way — including for the creators we personally watch, and especially for the ones with the loudest defenders.

This page is the cover of an ongoing record. The rankings change daily as new candles close. The archive does not. If a creator appears here and dislikes their score, they are welcome to make better calls. The Ledger will be waiting.
