01 · framing

Five desktop screens, two new viewports.

scope · @375 · @768
desktop · @1440 unchanged
screens · Leaderboard · Profile · Signals · Compare · Dashboard

CTR is a desktop-first product — the decision-cockpit density, the ETH denominator reconciler, the 4-way Compare all assume two-column layouts with room to breathe. But discovery happens on phones: a Twitter link to a creator's profile, a push alert about an α drop, a quick "is this signal live?" check between meetings. Mobile has to carry the same semantic weight without pretending to be the desktop.

This pass isn't a visual redesign. It's a compression ruleset: which primitives survive as-is, which reflow, which get replaced by a mobile-native pattern, which are explicitly dropped below a breakpoint. The goal is that a user arriving on a phone still gets trust · evidence · synthesis in that order — never a table with horizontal scroll.

v0.1 note · I'm junior-reasoning this pass slide-by-slide. Before each screen, I state the core tension that mobile surfaces. If the tension is wrong, the mocks are wrong — flag at the framing layer, not the pixel layer.

Three breakpoints total. Not four, not five — the product has three meaningful layouts and pretending it has more creates dead zones.

convention
Mobile-first CSS, min-width media queries. Base styles = phone. Additive @media (min-width: 768px) adds tablet. Additive @media (min-width: 1280px) adds desktop. No max-width queries — they create brittle in-between states when a new breakpoint lands.
between 1280 – 1440
Desktop layout shown at natural width, max-width: 1360px container (as today). Below 1280 the tablet rules fire — not a gradual squeeze of the desktop grid.
above 1440
Container caps at 1360px centered. Whitespace gets larger, content doesn't. We don't do "ultrawide" variants.
tokens wired
bp.phone · bp.tab · bp.desk — registered alongside D5's metric.* family. Token total: 24 → 27.
03 · ruleset

What survives, what reflows, what dies.

primitives · 14
kept · 6 · reflow · 5
replaced · 2 · dropped · 1

This is the ruleset the five screen designs will apply. Named before drawing so the mocks can't quietly invent new patterns.

primitive@375@768mobile behavior
01 α value + CI band keep keep The headline number. Stays readable at 14/1.4 on phone, 16/1.4 on tablet. CI band narrows but never disappears — if space is too tight, shrink the band to a pill, never collapse to ±.
02 Tier badge · S/A/B/C keep keep Badge is 3 chars max; fits anywhere. Keep at every viewport.
03 Originator glyph keep keep 10px pip + 1-char label. Invented precisely for density — mobile is where it earns its keep.
04 Evidence strip (SRC/N/CN/TM) hold keep 4-glyph strip fits tablet fine. On phone it stays but shows only 2 glyphs by default (SRC + N); the other two reveal on tap. Horizontal scroll would mislead — it'd imply there's more to read when there's just more glyphs.
05 Metric hover popover (D5) → sheet keep Replaced on phone with bottom sheet. Same 4 slots (What / How / Good / Source) stacked vertically. ℹ︎ button becomes the sheet's footer action. Hover is a desktop gesture — forcing it into a long-press on phone is user-hostile.
06 Decision cockpit (Dashboard) reflow reflow 1×4 row on desktop → 2×2 grid on tablet → vertical list of 4 on phone. Semantic order preserved: α / movement / names-missing / route-trace. Each cell full-width, hairline-separated.
07 Leaderboard table → cards reflow Replaced on phone with card list (rank · handle · α · tier · pip row). On tablet, table stays but drops columns in priority order: β-α → H → V → OL dropped first. Never horizontal scroll.
08 Compare 2-way / 4-way reflow reflow Synthesis-first discipline survives. 4-way on phone = 2-way + pager (swipe for pair 3–4). 3-way on tablet only. Synthesis headline always above the fold, side-by-side metrics collapse to stacked.
09 Signals skim list reflow keep On phone, each signal row grows to 3-line card (headline · evidence strip · synthesis fragment). On tablet, near-identical to desktop with narrower right column.
10 Assumptions panel → drawer keep Replaced on phone with a pull-up drawer accessed from footer; on tablet, it collapses but sidebar-docks. Assumptions are essential but not primary — they never block the fold.
11 Methodology drawer (D7 owed) keep keep Full-page drawer on every viewport. Content-layout concern, not density concern — same treatment top-down.
12 Pricing page (D4) reflow reflow 3 tier cards stack vertically on phone; 2-col on tablet. Upgrade modals stay modal-center on tablet, become full-screen on phone. Team tier card stays collapsed by default at every viewport.
13 Top masthead reflow reflow Wordmark + hamburger on phone; wordmark + primary nav (4 items) + avatar on tablet. Breadcrumb only appears on desktop. Sticky at every viewport.
14 Sticky left rail (Signals filter) drop reflow Dropped on phone — filters collapse into a single bottom-sheet "Filter" action. On tablet it becomes a collapsible top filter bar, not a sidebar.
04 · S1 leaderboard

Tension · table is the primitive, ranking is the intent.

desktop · 8-col table
phone · card list · tablet · 4-col table

Leaderboard's desktop design is a dense rankable table — 8 columns, hover-to-explore. Mobile kills horizontal scroll, so the question is: does the user need to rank, or to orient? My read: on phone they're orienting (who is this creator, can I trust them). On tablet they're doing both. On desktop they're ranking.

This separation is why the phone version is cards, not a compressed table. A card lets the most important primitives (α, tier, pip row) breathe without rewarding horizontal scroll.

@375 · phone rank hidden (shown on tap)
α + tier + pips
card list, tap → profile
Why cards, not table: tables imply comparability row-to-row. On a 375 screen you can only see 4–5 rows at once — not enough to compare. Cards reframe the screen as "browse creators" instead of "rank creators."

05 · S2 dashboard

Tension · cockpit density vs. one-decision-per-screen.

desktop · 1×4 cockpit
tablet · 2×2 · phone · stacked list

The Dashboard decision-cockpit is the most spatially-ambitious thing in the product — four semantic cells, synthesized at a glance. The glance is desktop-shaped. On phone, you cannot glance at four things; you can glance at one. So the cockpit reflows to a vertical sequence, preserving semantic order, preserving the "what changed since last time" framing, but abandoning the 4-up grid.

On tablet, 2×2 works — it's the only viewport where the grid is worth defending.

@375 · phone stacked 4-cell list
full-width cards
semantic order preserved
bottom-nav Dashboard tab active
Never a 2×2 on phone: two cockpit cells per row at 375px = 170px each. Cell 4's route-trace value needs ≥240px to not truncate. Truncating a route-trace is worse than stacking.

06 · S3 metric sheet

Tension · hover is desktop, long-press is hostile.

desktop · hover popover (D5)
tablet · tap popover · phone · bottom sheet

The D5 popover assumes hover — a fundamentally desktop input. Forcing long-press on mobile is user-hostile; people don't know it's there. On tablet, tap-popover works because there's room for the popover to anchor without occluding the triggering number. On phone, there is no such room — the popover would cover the number it explains. So the phone pattern is a bottom sheet: same 4 slots (What / How / Good / Source), vertically stacked, dismissible by swipe-down.

Critical constraint: the triggering metric stays visible above the sheet. If you have to dismiss the sheet to see what you were inspecting, the design failed.

@375 · phone bottom sheet · ~60% height
4 slots stacked
ℹ︎ becomes footer action
swipe-down to dismiss
Height rule: sheet never exceeds 70% viewport. The metric row stays above the sheet. If the full 4 slots don't fit at 70%, the Good slot's scale visualization collapses to inline text before the sheet grows.

07 · S4 compare

Tension · synthesis-first, even at 375.

desktop · 2-way · 4-way
tablet · 2-way · 3-way · phone · 2-way + pager

Compare's entire point is the synthesis headline — what differs, not what's listed. That discipline has to survive on phone even harder than on desktop, because phone rewards list-first patterns. We don't list voices; we state the asymmetry, then offer the evidence.

4-way Compare doesn't exist on phone. If you started a 4-way on desktop and opened the link on phone, it becomes a 2-way (first pair) with a pager dot showing "pair 2 of 2 · swipe". On tablet, 3-way exists as max — 4-way would cram.

@375 · phone synthesis above the fold
metric pairs stack vertically
4-way → 2-way + pager
no side-by-side columns
Synthesis headline hard rule: above-fold on every viewport. If it's not visible on first paint, Compare degenerates to "two profiles stacked" — which is not Compare, that's the browser's back button.

08 · S5 signals

Tension · the filter rail is desktop furniture.

desktop · left filter rail
tablet · top filter bar · phone · filter sheet

Signals' sticky left rail — active filters, asset chips, time window — is desktop's gift. On tablet it survives as a horizontal top bar (collapsible). On phone it must collapse entirely into a single "Filter" button that opens a sheet — otherwise it eats the fold that the signal list needs.

Each signal row grows vertically on phone: a 3-line card (headline · evidence strip · synthesis fragment) is better than a 1-line table row at 14px. Skim quality is preserved by spacing, not by density.

@375 · phone filter button top-right
signals as 3-line cards
2-glyph evidence strip
synthesis fragment per card
2-glyph default: the evidence strip shows SRC + N; CN and TM reveal on tap. On a 375 card, 4 glyphs create a horizontal rhythm that competes with the headline. Two doesn't.
