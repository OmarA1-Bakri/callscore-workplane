Five ownable product primitives.

§14 metric · §7 trust · §13 states · §16 conversion

These primitives are where the product expression lives. Each is specified once, then deployed consistently across the screens. The goal is recognition — a user should identify a CTR screen on a glance because of how Alpha Score, confidence, provenance, freshness, and lock read. Not decoration; each primitive earns its place by answering a §7 trust question or a §14 "how good is this number" question.

The rule
  • The accent-filled square is "trust-verified". It means this datum has a canonical source and a click resolves to that source.

Three scales

Signal freshness
Age-first, magnitude-second. A §9 freshness vs noise resolution: quiet when stable, ringed when <24h.

§5D freshness
§13 new · stale

The three states
  • Hot (<24h) — accent pulse, concentric ring animation. One signal can be "hot" at a time visually — if multiple, they de-escalate oldest-first.
  • Fresh (24h–72h) — static dot in --new. No animation.
  • Stale (>72h) — stale color, timestamp label prefixed with "stale".
Copy directive
  • Relative + absolute, always paired. "4h ago · 2026-04-24 10:12 UTC". Never relative alone (timezone ambiguity).
  • Labels: "detected", "forming", "consensus", "fading", "invalidated" — verbs. No adjectives like "strong" or "weak" (that's what confidence-% is for).
