CF-A · Resolution logic

β math in v0.2 did not reconcile — choosing the clean log-return spread.

The v0.2 "β stated" cleanup walked through a worked example that did not arithmetically close: with β = 1.18 and benchmark return +7.2% on a +14.98% SOL move, the β-adjusted excess resolves to +6.48%, not the stated +12.10%. Two paths were offered; we're choosing Option 1.

α  =  ln(Pᵣ / Pₑ) − ln(Pᵦ,ᵣ / Pᵦ,ₑ)
asset log-return − benchmark log-return

SOL · Pₑ $142.80 · Pᵣ $164.20 · ln(164.20/142.80) = +13.96%
BTC · Pᵦ,ₑ $62,140 · Pᵦ,ᵣ $66,820 · ln(66,820/62,140) = +7.26%
α = +13.96% − +7.26% = +6.70%
stated on all resolved rows as α = +6.70
1 Drop β; use the clean log-return spread. Users can verify against spot prices. No opaque rolling-β assumption. This is the form that landed cleanly in P2 v0.1. CHOSEN. chosen
2 Keep β, specify it as rolling per-asset, and publish the worked example with reconciled arithmetic. Analytically stronger but heavier to explain and audit. rejected
Decision. The simple log-return spread is the default. β-adjusted α lives as a separate paid column ("β-α") on resolved tables and on the Score Explanation drawer — never replacing α silently. This keeps the free tier auditable against a calculator while giving the paid tier the more defensible number.

CF-B · CI-vs-label tension

The drawer says rank 01↔02 sits in the 95% CI. The label says "Top performer."

The Score Explanation drawer publishes a confidence interval that honestly shows rank 01 and rank 02 often sit inside each other's intervals. Meanwhile leaderboard rows, profile heroes, and tier chips read as unconditional ("Top performer · tier S"). That's a semantic conflict: the drawer tells the user the rank is noisy; the chrome tells them it isn't. This needs a product decision — not a design-only fix.

1 Drawer caveat only. Chrome labels stay firm ("Top performer"). Simplest; least honest. Tradeoff: preserves editorial punch, but the CI story exists in one place only and most users never open the drawer. not rec
2 Tier-named labels + CI micro-annotation on ranks. Label reads "Tier S · rank #01" with a small "±1 within CI" hint next to the rank number on leaderboard + profile hero. Tier is defensible (stable); rank is disclaimed where it matters. RECOMMENDED. rec
3 CI-aware treatment on every metric card. Every rank/metric carries ± bars. Most honest; most visually heavy. Risk: noise crowds out the editorial signal. Tradeoff: trust-maximal but undermines "five-second job-leads rule." not rec
Recommendation · Option 2. Landing in P3 leaderboard + profile as a provisional treatment (visible in the Signals/Dashboard/Compare screens below), pending product sign-off. If product picks 1 or 3 instead, the change is contained to two shared primitives — not thirty screens. Raise at Phase 3 review.
D1 · §5D · §4.4

Signals — the consensus page, not a second leaderboard.

surface 01 / 05
states shown · 9
viewport · 1440

Signals answers a different question than the leaderboard. Leaderboard asks who should I trust; Signals asks what are trusted creators saying right now, and are they agreeing. The unit is a thesis — an asset + direction with a short editorial summary — not a creator and not a single call. A thesis aggregates across creators who are calling the same trade in the same window, and carries its own lifecycle chips §4.4: forming · consensus · fading · invalid. Free tier sees consensus that has had time to form (~24h); Live Signals (<6h, per-creator attribution, push alerts) is paid §16.2.

The page splits into active / resolved because the audit story matters here even more than on the leaderboard — if we show "consensus: SOL long" today, we must show the same thesis's outcome 30 days later. The resolved table uses the same α, β-α, confidence-interval, and retraction-diamond primitives committed in P2 v0.2 §5B · §5C.

§9 · Tension raised

Signals page shows consensus. Leaderboard penalizes herd calls.

Scoring rewards creators whose calls are resolved correctly, not who was loudest on consensus. But Signals surfaces exactly the theses where creators are agreeing — which also tend to be the most consensus-safe, lowest-conviction calls a creator can make. A scoring rule that silently penalizes consensus participation will pull top tier voices away from this page, which is the page free users land on. We need to decide whether α carries an originality weight.

Working resolution
Keep α free of an originality weight. Add an "originator" glyph next to creators on the voices stack who published the thesis before it became consensus (first-mover within ±6h). Surface first-mover rate as a profile secondary metric §5C. Do not adjust scoring silently. Decision deferred to Phase 4 · scoring review.
