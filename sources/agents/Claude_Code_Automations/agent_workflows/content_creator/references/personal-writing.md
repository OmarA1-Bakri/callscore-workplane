# Personal Writing Guide

## Purpose

This reference governs long-form writing for Omar's personal properties:

- `omarbakri.com`
- `intelligentrails.news`

These pieces are personal essays and newsletters about the intersection of AI, FinTech, business strategy, sales, leadership, and career judgment.

They are NOT product collateral.
They are NOT TheGent sales material.
They are NOT ICP-nurture content written as if the reader is already a buyer.

## Audience

Write for smart operators:

- founders
- sales leaders
- partnership leaders
- executives in FinTech and financial services
- ambitious professionals thinking about strategy, growth, and technology

The reader is commercially literate and time-poor. They want a sharp point of view, not a brochure.

## Voice

- Conversational, but controlled
- Senior and thoughtful, not performative
- Opinionated without sounding theatrical
- Precise, with clean sentences and clear rhythm
- Personal when useful, not autobiographical for its own sake

## What Good Looks Like

- Start with a real tension, not a generic topic introduction
- Make one central claim and stay with it
- Use business judgment, not buzzwords, as the source of authority
- Sound like someone who has sat in real meetings, carried targets, and made decisions under pressure
- Leave the reader with a sharper way to think, not just a summary of the issue

## Strong Topic Areas

- AI as a management and decision-making tool
- enterprise sales and partnerships
- strategy versus activity
- judgment, taste, and leadership
- operating inside complex industries
- technology adoption in financial services
- how ambitious people think about careers, leverage, and positioning

## Avoid

- product promotion
- TheGent-specific messaging unless explicitly requested
- newsletter copy that sounds like campaign content
- generic inspiration
- excessive frameworks, bullets, or listicles unless the subject genuinely needs them
- writing that sounds generated, polished-but-empty, or over-explained

## Structural Bias

Prefer essay structure over marketing structure:

1. sharp opening tension
2. clear claim
3. development through reasoning and selective examples
4. strong ending that lands the point

## Style Checks

- If the same idea appears in three adjacent paragraphs, compress it
- If the draft could be said by any AI/tech founder on LinkedIn, it is too generic
- If the piece sounds like a company newsletter, it has failed
- If the point of view is weak, sharpen the thesis before polishing prose
